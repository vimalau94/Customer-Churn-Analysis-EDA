📊 **Customer Churn Analysis – Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**
📌 **Project Overview**

Customer churn is a critical business problem where customers stop using a company’s services. This project performs an exploratory data analysis (EDA) on a telecom customer dataset to identify key factors driving customer churn and to provide actionable business insights for improving customer retention.

The analysis focuses on customer demographics, tenure, contract type, service usage, and payment methods, supported by clear visualizations and percentage-based insights.

🎯 **Objective**

The main objectives of this project are:

To analyze customer churn patterns

To identify high-risk customer segments

To understand how contract types, payment methods, tenure, and services affect churn

To provide data-driven recommendations for reducing churn

📂 **Dataset Information**

Dataset Name: Telco Customer Churn

File: Customer Churn.csv

Records: ~7,000 customers

Target Variable: Churn (Yes / No)

**Key Features:**

Demographics: Gender, SeniorCitizen

Account Info: Tenure, Contract, PaymentMethod

Services: InternetService, OnlineSecurity, TechSupport, StreamingTV

Billing: MonthlyCharges, TotalCharges

🛠️ **Tools & Technologies Used**

Python

Pandas – data manipulation

Matplotlib & Seaborn – data visualization

Jupyter Notebook – analysis environment

🔍 **Analysis Performed**

The following analyses were carried out:

Data cleaning and preprocessing

Univariate analysis (distribution of churn)

Bivariate analysis using bar charts and stacked bar charts

Percentage-based churn comparison across categories

Visualization-driven insight generation

Several charts were created to clearly show churn behavior across different customer segments.

📈 **Key Insights & Findings**
🔹 Overall Churn

Approximately 26–27% of customers have churned

Around 73–74% customers are retained

🔹 Contract Type & Churn

Month-to-month contracts: ~42% churn rate

One-year contracts: ~11% churn rate

Two-year contracts: ~3% churn rate

📌 Long-term contracts significantly reduce churn.

🔹 Payment Method & Churn

Electronic Check: ~45% churn rate (highest)

Credit Card / Bank Transfer / Mailed Check: ~15–18% churn rate

📌 Customers using automated or secure payment methods are more stable.

🔹 Tenure & Churn

Less than 1 year tenure: ~50% churn

1–3 years tenure: ~35% churn

More than 3 years: ~15% churn

📌 Early-stage customers are the most vulnerable.

🔹 Senior Citizen Analysis

Senior Citizens: ~41% churn rate

Non-Senior Customers: ~26% churn rate

📌 Senior customers require targeted retention strategies.

🔹 Internet Service Type

Fiber Optic users: ~30% churn

DSL users: ~20% churn

📌 Higher churn among fiber users may indicate pricing or service expectation issues.

📊 **Visualizations**

The project includes:

Count plots for churn distribution

Stacked bar charts showing percentage-based churn

Comparative visualizations across contract types, payment methods, tenure, and services

These visualizations help translate raw data into clear business insights.

✅ **Business Recommendations**

Based on the analysis:

Encourage customers to switch to long-term contracts

Promote auto-payment methods over electronic checks

Improve onboarding and engagement in the first year

Introduce special retention programs for senior citizens

Bundle value-added services to reduce churn risk
